# Dyrt Series A — 60-Day Close Playbook

**Window: May 14 – August 14, 2026 | Target: $20M | Lead check: $3–5M**

---

## The Strategy

A $20M Series A with a $3–5M lead needs one conviction investor to anchor — then the rest fills fast with the tailwind of a signed term sheet. The playbook has three tracks running in parallel:

1. **Lead Track** — 5–6 high-conviction targets, push to term sheet by Day 35
2. **NM Track** — NMSIC-backed funds with NM facility mandate, run separately
3. **Follow-on Track** — Warm relationships and re-engages filling the $15M balance

## The Objection Playbook

### "Too much hardware"
> "The physical ops layer is the moat, not the cost center. Every competitor who tried software-only failed on verification — you can't report what you can't physically track. Our hardware creates the data; the data creates the business."

### "Too early / need more ARR"
> "Noro-Moseley invests at $2–10M ARR — we're at $1M and growing 450% YoY. This is the right moment: proven product-market fit, 0% churn, and $38M of signed or near-signed pipeline that converts to ARR in the next 12 months."

### "Can't do uncapped notes" (Regeneration VC)
> "We're running a priced Series A — no uncapped notes. This is a clean equity round."

### "Software only"
> "Pitch the SaaS and data platform exclusively. The Intelligent Platform tier ($500/mo) with AI bill ingestion, compliance exports, and Scope 3 dashboards stands alone. Physical ops is bundled only for full-service customers."

### "Check size too small to lead"
> "We're looking for a $3–5M lead to anchor — if you can write $1–2M, that's perfect co-investor sizing. We'll have the lead locked in first and would love [firm] alongside."

### "Board control" (Cove Fund — do not re-engage)
> Non-starter. Confirmed pass.
